
# Business Requirements Document (BRD)

## 1. Executive Summary
[Provide a high-level overview of the project’s purpose, objectives, and business benefits.]

## 2. Project Background
[Describe the business need or challenge that prompted the project. Provide context, any historical information, and relevant market factors.]

## 3. Business Objectives
- [Clearly state the key objectives that this project will achieve.]

## 4. Scope
### 4.1 In Scope
- [List the business areas or functions that will be impacted.]

### 4.2 Out of Scope
- [List any areas that will not be affected by this project.]

## 5. Stakeholders
[List key stakeholders including business owners, sponsors, and end users.]

## 6. Business Requirements
[High-level business needs that are to be addressed. These are often more abstract and less detailed than functional requirements.]

### 6.1 Functional Overview
- [Describe what the solution should do from a business perspective.]

## 7. Constraints and Assumptions
- [List business constraints and high-level assumptions relevant to project delivery.]

## 8. Risk Assessment
[Highlight potential business risks and provide high-level mitigation strategies.]

## 9. Success Criteria
[Define how business success will be measured post-project completion.]

## 10. Timeline and Milestones
[Indicate the high-level project timeline with important dates or milestones.]

## 11. Budget Overview
[Provide a high-level estimation of the budget.]

## 12. Approvals
[Include signatures or approval documentation from key stakeholders.]
